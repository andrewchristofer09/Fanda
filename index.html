Mental Health

"Is a state of mental well-being that enables people to cope with the stresses of life, realize their abilities, learn well and work well, and contribute to their community."

World Health Organization (WHO).

Many people with a mental health disorder have more than one condition at a time. It is important to note that good mental health depends on a delicate balance of factors and that several elements may contribute to developing these disorders.

SDG Target 3.4

aims to reduce premature mortality from non-communicable diseases through prevention and treatment and promote mental health and well-being.

Next

Some examples of non-communicable diseases in mental health :

  • Anxiety
  • OCD (obsessive-compulsive disorder)
  • Depression
  • Schizophrenia
  • Bipolar disorder

Non-communicable diseases are frequently encountered in patients with severe mental disorders such as schizophrenia.
